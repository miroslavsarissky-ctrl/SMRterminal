## The distinction that matters most
Separate three registers and tag every item by which it is. Do not present in-kind access as if it were money.
- **cash** — real, competitive, usually cost-shared money (e.g. ARPA-E NOFOs, NEUP). Note that these fund research, not build-out.
- **inkind** — access, material or partnership with no cash to the applicant (e.g. GAIN vouchers give lab access not money; CRADAs are in-kind partnerships; material-allocation programmes hand over material while the applicant funds the facility; resource-access FOAs provide sites/material/expertise with the applicant carrying every dollar).
- **allocated** — money that exists but is essentially spoken for (e.g. ARDP). Reference only.

## Where to look
Verify each on the sponsor's own site. Prefer: ARPA-E eXCHANGE (arpa-e-foa.energy.gov) for NOFOs and teaming partner lists; DOE Office of Nuclear Energy (energy.gov/ne) for programmes and RFAs; GAIN (gain.inl.gov) for voucher rounds and their deadlines; NEUP (neup.inl.gov); SAM.gov and Grants.gov for RFIs, sources-sought and NOFOs; DOE lab technology-transfer pages for CRADA mechanisms. Highest priority topics: transuranic/recycled fuels, MOX and plutonium disposition, spent-fuel recycling and closed fuel cycles, advanced/fast-reactor fuels and materials, fuel fabrication, and lead-cooled materials qualification.

## Discipline

Label anything you cannot confirm on an official source as Low confidence. Fewer, well-verified entries beat a padded list.
